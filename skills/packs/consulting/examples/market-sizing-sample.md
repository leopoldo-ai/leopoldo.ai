# Market Sizing: European AI Consulting Market
**Methodology:** Top-down + Bottom-up triangulation
**Geography:** EU27 + UK
**Base year:** 2025
**Horizon:** 2025-2030

---

## Methodology

**Top-down approach:** Starting from total European management consulting spend (Eurostat + FEACO data: ~€42B in 2025), applying AI-related project share (currently ~14%, rising) and professional services AI premium multiplier.

**Bottom-up approach:** Estimating from supply side. Number of active consulting firms with AI practices in Europe (est. 4,200 firms), average revenue per AI consultant per year (€180K-€320K blended), utilization and project mix applied to derive addressable revenue pool.

Both approaches are triangulated against analyst reports (IDC, Gartner) and cross-checked against disclosed revenue of publicly listed European consulting groups.

## Growth Drivers

- **Enterprise AI adoption acceleration.** EU AI Act compliance deadlines (2025-2027) are forcing large enterprises to engage external consultants for risk classification, system audits, and governance frameworks. This is a non-discretionary spend category.
- **Talent gap.** Internal AI capability buildout is lagging demand. 73% of European CIOs report insufficient internal AI expertise (IDC 2025). Consulting fills the gap while internal hiring catches up.
- **LLM-to-workflow integration demand.** The market has shifted from "AI strategy" (2022-2024) to "AI implementation" (2025+). Workflow redesign, agent deployment, and system integration projects command higher fees and longer engagements.
- **Regulatory tailwind.** GDPR enforcement on AI systems, the EU AI Act, and sector-specific rules (DORA in financial services, MDR in health) are creating a recurring compliance consulting cycle with high renewal rates.

## Key Takeaways

- The European AI consulting market is entering a high-growth phase driven by mandatory regulatory compliance and enterprise implementation demand. SAM growth at 23% CAGR outpaces total consulting growth by 3x.
- AI-native consulting firms are best positioned to capture disproportionate SOM share. Their advantage is speed and tooling, not headcount. The competitive window before traditional Big 4 firms fully retool is 18-36 months.
- Bottom-up and top-down estimates converge within 8%, validating the €5.9B SAM figure for 2025 as a reliable planning baseline.
